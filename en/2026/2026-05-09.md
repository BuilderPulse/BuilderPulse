### With 2 hours today or a full weekend, what should I build?

**🔍 Signal**: The strongest software-first wedge is the 258-comment [actual UUID v4 collision](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48060054) thread, reinforced by comments about weak entropy, frontend generation, deterministic browser execution, and today's broader demand for proof around hidden software assumptions.

**Best 2-hour build**: UUID Entropy Canary, a CI and browser-runtime report that samples ID generation across your backend, frontend, test runner, worker, and crawler-like environment, then fails loudly if duplicates, low entropy, or deterministic outputs appear.

**Fastest validation step**: If you want to validate this today, start with a public repo that runs the canary against `crypto.randomUUID()`, `uuid`, a browser page, and a Node worker, then reply to the HN thread with the first surprising result.

### What is today's most counter-intuitive finding?

**🔍 Signal**: The most useful builder idea did not come from the largest threads; it came from a 258-comment UUID collision discussion while the bigger boards were dominated by layoffs, geopolitics, outages, and cultural nostalgia.

**Takeaway**: Hunt for false assumptions, not just hot categories; "this should never happen" is a strong product prompt when the check is cheap.

**Counter-view**: Some rare-bug products are too narrow, so the landing page must connect the check to data integrity and incident avoidance.
